The Living Library

Unveiling the 66 Books of the Bible

Journey through the inspired collection of ancient texts that form the foundation of faith, history, and wisdom. Discover the essence of each book, from creation to revelation.

Old Testament (39 Books)

Genesis

Creation, the Fall, Noah, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, Joseph. Origins of humanity and Israel.

Exodus

Moses, the Ten Plagues, liberation from Egypt, Red Sea, Ten Commandments, Tabernacle.

Leviticus

Laws for holy living, sacrifices, priesthood. Guidelines for worship and moral purity.

Numbers

Israel's wilderness journey, census, rebellion, Balaam's prophecies. From Sinai to Moab.

Deuteronomy

Moses' final speeches, restating the Law before entering Canaan. Call to remember and obey.

Joshua

Conquest of Canaan, division of land among tribes. God's faithfulness to His promises.

Judges

Cycle of sin, oppression, repentance, and deliverance through judges. Chaos before kings.

Ruth

Loyalty, love, and redemption story of a Moabitess. Ancestor of King David.

1 Samuel

Birth of Samuel, rise of King Saul, anointing of David. Transition from judges to monarchy.

2 Samuel

King David's reign, victories, sins, and family struggles. The Davidic Covenant.

1 Kings

Solomon's reign, temple built, kingdom divides. Elijah's prophetic ministry begins.

2 Kings

Divided kingdoms of Israel and Judah, Elisha, decline, and eventual exile to Babylon.

1 Chronicles

Genealogies, focus on David's reign and preparations for the Temple.

2 Chronicles

Solomon's reign, the Temple, history of Judah's kings, exile, and hope of return.

Ezra

Return from Babylonian exile, rebuilding the Temple in Jerusalem. Emphasis on covenant renewal.

Nehemiah

Rebuilding Jerusalem's walls, social and religious reforms. Leadership and perseverance.

Esther

Courage of Queen Esther saving Jews from genocide in Persia. God's hidden providence.

Job

The problem of suffering and God's justice. Dialogue between Job and his friends.

Psalms

A collection of songs and prayers expressing praise, lament, wisdom, and prophecy.

Proverbs

Practical wisdom for daily living, moral instruction, fear of the Lord.

Ecclesiastes

Philosophical exploration of life's meaning, vanity, and finding joy in God.

Song of Solomon

Poetic celebration of romantic love between a bride and groom. Allegory of divine love.

Isaiah

Major prophet. Prophecies of judgment, comfort, the Messiah's coming, and salvation.

Jeremiah

Major prophet. Warnings to Judah before exile, laments, and new covenant promises.

Lamentations

Poetic expression of grief over Jerusalem's destruction by Babylon.

Ezekiel

Major prophet in exile. Visions of God's glory, judgment on Israel, and restoration.

Daniel

Faithful Jew in Babylonian court, prophetic visions of empires and God's ultimate kingdom.

Hosea

Minor prophet. God's unwavering love for unfaithful Israel, portrayed through Hosea's marriage.

Joel

Minor prophet. Locust plague as a warning, call to repentance, promise of the Spirit.

Amos

Minor prophet. Prophecies against social injustice and hypocrisy in Israel and surrounding nations.

Obadiah

Minor prophet. Prophecy of judgment against Edom for their pride and malice toward Jacob.

Jonah

Minor prophet. Reluctant prophet sent to Nineveh, God's compassion for all nations.

Micah

Minor prophet. Prophecies against injustice, promise of a ruler from Bethlehem (Jesus).

Nahum

Minor prophet. Prophecy of God's wrath and the complete destruction of Nineveh.

Habakkuk

Minor prophet. Prophet questions God about injustice, God reveals His plan of using Babylon, faith.

Zephaniah

Minor prophet. Prophecy of the "Day of the Lord," judgment and future restoration.

Haggai

Minor prophet. Encourages returned exiles to rebuild the Temple, emphasizing God's priority.

Zechariah

Minor prophet. Visions encouraging Temple rebuilding, prophecies of the Messiah's coming.

Malachi

Minor prophet. Final Old Testament prophet, addresses Israel's spiritual apathy, foretells John the Baptist.

New Testament (27 Books)

Matthew

Gospel. Presents Jesus as the promised Messiah, King of the Jews, fulfilling Old Testament prophecy.

Mark

Gospel. Fast-paced account of Jesus' ministry, miracles, and suffering as the Servant of God.

Luke

Gospel. Detailed account of Jesus' life, ministry, compassion for all, especially the marginalized.

John

Gospel. Focuses on Jesus' divine nature, "I AM" statements, and the path to eternal life through belief.

Acts

History of the early church, spread of the Gospel through the apostles, especially Peter and Paul.

Romans

Paul's epistle. Explains salvation by grace through faith, righteousness, and living a transformed life.

1 Corinthians

Paul's epistle. Addresses divisions, immorality, spiritual gifts, and resurrection in the Corinthian church.

2 Corinthians

Paul's epistle. Paul defends his apostleship, discusses suffering, generosity, and reconciliation.

Galatians

Paul's epistle. Argues against legalism, emphasizing freedom in Christ and justification by faith alone.

Ephesians

Paul's epistle. Explores God's eternal plan of salvation, unity in Christ, and spiritual warfare.

Philippians

Paul's epistle. A letter of joy and encouragement, emphasizing humility, unity, and finding joy in Christ.

Colossians

Paul's epistle. Warns against false teachings, exalts Christ as supreme, calls for holy living.

1 Thessalonians

Paul's epistle. Encouragement, teaching on Christ's return (rapture), and living a sanctified life.

2 Thessalonians

Paul's epistle. Clarifies teachings on Christ's return, warns against idleness, and encourages perseverance.

1 Timothy

Paul's pastoral epistle. Instructions for church leadership, worship, and conduct for Timothy.
